# Ghana Banking Sector Performance Analysis (2015-2023)

Python analysis of Ghana's banking sector performance using data from Bank of Ghana Annual Reports, covering 2015 to 2023.

## Project Overview

This notebook examines how Ghana's banking sector evolved over 8 years, with a focus on the 2017-2018 banking sector cleanup. The Bank of Ghana collapsed and merged several undercapitalised banks during this period, reducing the number of licensed banks from 34 to 23.

The analysis tracks sector-wide trends across assets, deposits, loans, capital adequacy, non-performing loans, and profitability.

## Key Findings

- Total banking sector assets grew from GHS 71.1Bn in 2015 to GHS 298.4Bn in 2023, a 320% increase
- NPL ratio peaked at 21.6% in 2017, the highest in the analysis period
- Capital adequacy ratio dropped to 11.8% in 2017, below the 13% regulatory minimum
- 34 licensed banks were reduced to 23 following the 2017-2018 cleanup
- ROE collapsed from 20.3% in 2016 to 8.4% in 2017, then recovered to 19.4% by 2021
- By 2023, total deposits reached GHS 214.7Bn and total loans reached GHS 98.6Bn
